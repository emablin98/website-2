About Me

Ema Blin is a french artist and filmmaker based in Tiohtià:ke/Montreal. They are currently pursuing a Masters of Fine Arts in Cinematic Arts at Concordia University. They hold a Bachelor’s of Fine Arts with Honours from Kingston University. Their current research focuses on the complex and disorienting experiences of transgender transformation through the medium of experimental film and animation. Their work challenges traditional narratives and aesthetic categories, particularly those associated with transitioning, by embracing themes of body horror and disintegration. Blin explores the visceral and raw aspects of bodily change, rejecting fixed identities and normative standards of beauty using their own experiences and theoretical frameworks to inform and shape their artistic output. Their research is not just about visual storytelling but also about asserting bodily autonomy and challenging binary epistemologies.
